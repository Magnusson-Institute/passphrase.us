Password/phrase Generator


This random password generator uses your physical movements to provide 'entropy'. Keep moving. Still need another 300 'events' from your fingers. You can move the mouse, press on the screen, or hammer at the keyboard.

We recommend you use the above password(s). They contain only letters and numbers that are easy to write down. Eight characters are enough for most cases. The question mark ? at the end is optional, and is only needed if you must have "at least one 'symbol'". If there is a 0 then that's the number zero.

If you need to memorize your password, you might prefer a passphrase instead. Three words is enough for most situations:

And finally, if you want as few keystrokes as possible, you can use this. It encodes using a variation of "base64", and comes in useful if you need stronger passwords. It will always have at least one number, one symbol, one uppercase, and one lowercase letter.

If you want to see alternatives to any of the above, just hit the button again. If you want to reset your "biometric entropy", you can re-load the page.


ADVANCED. The default settings above follow current best practices for most online situations: approximately 40 bits of entropy, picked by a machine rather than a human. That corresponds to eight 'base32' characters, or three words from our dictionary, or six 'base64' characters.
